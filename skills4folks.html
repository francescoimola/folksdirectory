In short

Three walk and talk meetups happening in Folkestone every last Thursday of the month across Summer 2025.

Trade what you're good at for the help you need‚Äîjoin Folkestone's budding and professional creatives for a slow stroll through town, followed by skill bartering and connecting over drinks at a local pub.

What's it all about

You know how it is: perhaps you ink beautiful tattoos but you've no clue on how to take great photos of your work. Equally, maybe you're a photographer who's keen to get a killer tattoo.

That's where bartering skills comes in:

Creatives helping each other out when money's tight or when you need a hand with something outside your wheelhouse. Enter Skills 4 Folks.

What to expect

A slow walk, chatting as we go and once at The Beer Shop, conversations can really get going over a pint. Clicked with someone? Swap illustration work for drum lessons, trade website tweaks for homemade sourdough or secure studio space in exchange for helping to mix an album. What you're willing to barter is up to you.

Expect a mixed crowd of artists, designers, writers, content creators, craftspeople, curators, gardeners, side-hustlers and more.

Facilitators

Sangmin, Stephen and Francesco are the collective behind Folks Directory, Folkestone's growing yellow pages for artists and creatives. What links them is a shared backgrounds in making art, facilitating creative work and organising grassroots events.

Sangmin is a curator and project manager with seven years of cross-cultural experience, recently boosting Folkestone's art scene. Sangmin has loads of curly hair.

Francesco is a designer at heart, but you can often find him building bridges across the murky, murky waters of marketing, strategy, and project facilitation.

Stephen is to blame for all the crocodiles, tigers and mini golf experiences you've probably seen or heard about in the last few years. He also teaches art and organises the Canterbury Exposure Open Mic.

Thanks

These events are part of the 2025 edition of Open Art Folke, a summer-and-a-bit-long festival of art and creativity from this gorgeous, little town by the sea. We owe a big thank you for their efforts in making this festival happen.

Independent and self-funded

Skills 4 Folks is an event run by the people behind Folks Directory: Folkestone's growing yellow pages for creative talent. Want to help us shape it? Donate some of your time to maintain the directory.
