Trade what you're good at for the help you need—join Folkestone's budding and professional creatives for a slow stroll through town, followed by skill bartering and connecting over drinks at a local pub.

What's it all about

You know how it is: perhaps you ink beatiful tattoos but you've no clue on how to take great photos of your work. Equally, maybe you're a photographer who's keen to get a killer tattoo.

That's where bartering skills comes in:

Creatives helping each other out when money's tight or when you need a hand with something outside your wheelhouse. Enter Skills 4 Folks.

What to expect

We kick things off with a walk through town (nothing strenuous, promise!) and chat as we go. We then head to The Beer Shop, where conversations can really get going over a pint. Clicked with someone? Swap illustration work for drum lessons, trade website tweaks for homemade sourdough or secure studio space in exchange for helping to mix an album. It's that simple.

Expect a mixed crowd of artists, designers, writers, content creators, craftspeople, curators, gardeners, side-hustlers with a creative hobby, and more.

Facilitators

Sangmin and Francesco are the independent duo behind Folks Directory, Folkestone’s growing yellow pages for artists and creatives. United by shared backgrounds in organising grassroots events, they're working to empower creative people to connect with one another for peer support.

Sangmin is a curator and project manager with seven years of cross-cultural experience, recently boosting Folkestone’s art scene. Sangmin has loads of curly hair.

Francesco is a designer at heart, but you can often find him building bridges across the murky, murky waters of marketing, strategy, and project facilitation.

Part of

These events are part of the 2025 edition of Open Art Folke, a summer-and-a-bit-long festival of art and creativity from this gorgeous, little town by the sea.

Independent and self-funded

Skills 4 Folks is an event run by Francesco and Sangmin, the team behind Folks Directory. Get in touch if you'd like to donate some of your time to maintain the directory.
